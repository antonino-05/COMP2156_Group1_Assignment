In Toronto, Ontario, Canada, there is a public institution called George Brown institution. 
Named for one of the Fathers of Confederation, George Brown, it was founded in 1967.


In a variety of subjects, including business, technology, health sciences, hospitality, and community services, the institution provides diploma, advanced diploma, degree, and postgraduate programs.

There are three major campuses of George Brown College:
The St. James Campus in Toronto's downtown
The Casa Loma Campus
Campus by the Waterfront

The college emphasizes industry-relevant skills and experiential, hands-on learning. Opportunities for field education, internships, and co-ops are included in many curricula.

George Brown is renowned for its solid relationships with business executives and employers, which enable students to obtain practical experience prior to graduation.



In order to prepare students for success in the Canadian workforce, George Brown places a strong emphasis on innovation, diversity, and career-focused education.
