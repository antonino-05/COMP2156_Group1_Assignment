The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) provides a structured approach for developing high-quality software.  

Typical SDLC stages include planning, analysis, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.  

DevOps improves SDLC by integrating development and operations teams, fostering collaboration and faster delivery.

Automation helps reduce human errors in testing and deployment processes.  

Continuous Integration allows frequent code merges and automated tests, while Continuous Deployment accelerates safe releases.  

Monitoring and feedback loops ensure applications remain reliable and improve over time.  

Understanding DevOps within SDLC helps students and professionals deliver robust software efficiently.
